# Quickarte App Audit — 2026-04-24

> Methodology note. The interactive browser tool was unavailable mid-audit, so
> this report is built from three evidence sources: (1) a live browser session
> earlier the same day that walked the full benchmark and produced a complete
> accessibility-snapshot trail in `.playwright-mcp/` (42 page snapshots from
> 12:42Z to 12:54Z, covering landing → register → onboarding → home → catalog
> → new-article → edit → storefront → order → confirmation → settings →
> logout → fresh Café onboarding → bad URLs), (2) HTTP probing of production
> routes for redirect/404 behaviour, and (3) source-code inspection to confirm
> root causes. Every finding below is traceable to at least one of these three.

## Summary
Quickarte has a solid QR-menu, onboarding, and storefront foundation, but it
is not shippable today for a pilot merchant who actually needs online
ordering. The dominant issue is an asymmetry: customers can submit orders on
every storefront, while the merchant's `/orders` page is gated behind an
entitlement they don't have — so a merchant can have a real order placed
against their business and no way to see it. On top of that, the product form
has click-blocking layout on mobile (the bottom nav eats taps on `Enregistrer`
and `+ Ajouter une option`), and the Google Places address collected in
onboarding is never surfaced back to the merchant. Top three wins: the
brutalist design language is consistent and genuinely on-brand, the
Boulangerie seed flow produces 20 good items across 5 categories in the
correct order, and the public storefront + checkout + confirmation path works
cleanly end-to-end for a customer.

## Works
- Public landing (`https://quickarte.fr/fr` → canonicalises to `/`) renders
  the real marketing page: hero "Le système de commerce des boulangeries et
  cafés qui se respectent", four-module grid (MENU & QR, COMMANDE EN LIGNE,
  FIDÉLITÉ, ANALYSES), pricing model section ("Vos commandes, votre argent"),
  contact, footer. Links are `/fr/login` and `/fr/register`.
- Public CTAs: "SE CONNECTER" reaches `/login`, "CRÉER MON ÉTABLISSEMENT"
  reaches `/register`.
- Registration: a throwaway user was created and redirected to
  `/onboarding`.
- Login: credentials from `.env.audit` log in cleanly and land on `/home`.
  Hitting `/login` while authenticated redirects back to `/home`.
- Logged-out protection: `/home`, `/catalog`, `/settings` all redirect to
  `/login` (HTTP 307 → 200 at `/login` on curl).
- Onboarding (`/onboarding`) is auth-gated, one page, French, mobile-first.
  Fields: `Nom de l'établissement`, 4-tile type grid
  (Boulangerie/Café/Restaurant/Autre), `Adresse` (Google Places autocomplete),
  `Adresse publique` slug with live preview. Submit is disabled until all
  required fields are valid; free text in the address field alone keeps it
  disabled — a Places suggestion must be selected.
- Boulangerie seed: choosing Boulangerie produces a catalog of 20 articles
  across 5 categories in the expected order:
  `01 / Viennoiseries`, `02 / Pains`, `03 / Pâtisseries`, `04 / Boissons`,
  `05 / Sandwichs & en-cas`. Sample prices observed: Croissant 1,30 €, Pain
  au chocolat 1,40 €, Baguette tradition 1,50 €, Brioche 3,50 €, Éclair au
  chocolat 3,50 €, Pain de campagne 4,50 €.
- Café seed rule: a fresh Café business starts with `0 articles` and an
  explicit empty state ("Aucun article dans votre catalogue" + "+ Ajouter
  votre premier article") — matches spec.
- `/home` renders: greeting ("Bonjour, <prénom>"), date, business name, QR
  code image, `Copier quickarte.fr/<slug>` button, `Télécharger le QR →`
  link (data URL PNG), an "Aujourd'hui" stat grid (Commandes + Revenu) once
  activity exists, and an Actions Rapides section ("Modifier le catalogue").
- `/catalog` lists categories and items in order, with per-item availability
  switches (checked by default) and an "Edit" link per item. Heading count
  ("20 articles") is accurate.
- Product create: via `/catalog/new`, keyboard-submitting the form saves an
  article and it appears in the chosen category with the entered price.
- Product edit: `/catalog/<id>/edit` saves name and price changes and the
  list reflects them. Also exposes a "Supprimer cet article" path (not
  executed to completion).
- Product availability toggle: flipping the switch hides the article from
  the public storefront.
- Inline category create: "+ Nouvelle catégorie" inside the product form
  creates a new category (`Audit Catégorie` was added and appeared in both
  the admin catalog and the storefront menu nav).
- `/settings` shows business profile section with Nom, Type (4-tile grid),
  Emplacement, Adresse publique (read-only). The expected caption is
  present verbatim: "Non modifiable pour le moment — changer l'adresse
  publique invaliderait vos QR codes."
- `/settings` edit: `Modifier →` switches Nom + Type to editable controls
  and `Enregistrer` persists. Renaming the business propagated to home and
  storefront.
- Public storefront at `/<slug>` (root path, not `/s/<slug>`): renders
  business name, category nav tabs, and all visible items with prices in
  EUR. Adding items opens an inline cart with "COMMANDER" CTA.
- Storefront checkout (`/<slug>/order`): item list with +/- quantity, "Mode"
  (Sur Place / À Emporter), table number when Sur Place, Nom + Téléphone,
  optional Notes, summary button "Passer Commande <N> ARTICLE <total>".
- Order confirmation (`/<slug>/order/confirmation`): displays order number
  (e.g. `#4E13B6C3`), status `En attente`, payment note `À régler sur
  place`, mode/table/client/phone/passée-le details, itemised list.
- Public 404: `/fr/doesnotexist` → `/doesnotexist` → custom French 404 with
  "404 / Page introuvable / Retour / Accueil" wording.
- Brutalist design language is consistent: `font-mono uppercase
  tracking-widest` labels, accent-yellow primary CTAs, black borders, mobile
  app shell (`max-w-[480px] mx-auto border-x border-outline/50`). Theme
  colour `#0A0A0A`. Favicon is served from `/favicon.svg` (HTTP 200).

## Broken / Buggy
- **Blocker — customer ordering while merchant order management is gated.**
  On `/<slug>/order` a customer can place an order (verified:
  confirmation page rendered with an order number). On `/orders` the
  merchant sees only `Module non activé` and a "Parler à Quickarte →"
  mailto link. The merchant cannot view, confirm, prepare, cancel, or
  complete that order from the UI. Severity: blocker for anyone piloting.
- **Major — `/home` still shows "Démarrage / Ajoutez votre premier article"
  after Boulangerie onboarding seeds 20 items.** Root cause in
  `app/[locale]/(merchant)/home/page.tsx:236`: the getting-started branch
  triggers when `todayOrderCount === 0 && todayRevenue === 0 && pendingCount
  === 0 && recentOrders.length === 0` — it never checks the catalog. So a
  fresh Boulangerie with a full seeded menu still gets the "add your first
  article" CTA until the first order arrives, which undermines the instant
  first-value promise.
- **Major — mobile product form has click-blocked controls.** On
  `/catalog/new`, clicking `Enregistrer` timed out in the browser session
  (pressing Enter worked). On `/catalog/<id>/edit`, clicking `+ Ajouter une
  option` also timed out. The fixed bottom nav (`Accueil` / `Catalogue`)
  overlays the form's bottom actions on narrow viewports. Severity: major
  because this is a core catalog task on the primary device class.
- **Major — onboarding address is not surfaced anywhere.** A Google Places
  suggestion was selected during onboarding, but `/settings` shows
  `Emplacement: Non renseignée`, and the storefront header carries no
  location. Either the address isn't being persisted, or it's persisted but
  not rendered — either way the merchant has no sign it was saved.
- **Major — revenue KPI is visually broken.** After a 1,30 € order, `/home`
  showed the label as "Revenu€" and the value as "1". Root cause in
  `lib/utils/currency.ts:42`: `formatAmountCompact` uses
  `maximumFractionDigits: 0`, so 1.30 rounds to 1. The `€` symbol is
  rendered inline with the label in `components/ui/stat-card.tsx`, producing
  "Revenu€". Severity: major for merchant trust (shows €1 when real revenue
  is €1,30).
- **Major — authed bad URLs fall through to Next's default English 404.**
  `/fr/catalog/nonexistent-id` eventually renders `404 / This page could
  not be found.` (captured in snapshots at 12:51Z and 12:54Z). Public
  `/fr/doesnotexist` renders the French custom 404 correctly, so this is
  specifically the merchant side falling through.
- **Minor — `/s/<slug>` returns 404.** Only the root path `/<slug>` is a
  valid storefront route. Since QR codes already point at the root, this is
  cosmetic, but anyone testing the documented `/s/<slug>` convention from
  the benchmark will see a broken link.
- **Minor — `/fr/…` paths canonicalise to non-locale paths
  (`/login`, `/register`, `/home`, …).** Works fine; just differs from the
  benchmark wording. Middleware rewrites `/fr` to `/` so the `[locale]`
  segment is effectively hidden in the URL bar.
- **Polish — `/favicon.ico` returns 404.** `/favicon.svg` exists and is
  referenced, but browsers that probe `favicon.ico` first will get a 404
  and a console error.

## Missing / Not implemented
- Merchant bottom nav is only two tabs (`Accueil`, `Catalogue`) for
  accounts without entitlements. `Commandes` and `Fidélité` are *completely
  hidden* from nav — a merchant has to know the URLs to reach the upsell
  screens.
- Order manager capabilities (pending/prepared/complete actions, cancel,
  type filters for dine-in / takeaway / click-and-collect). The live UI
  was the gated upsell card for every tested account.
- `click_and_collect` mode is not present in the customer checkout. Only
  `Sur Place` and `À Emporter` appear. Landing copy promises
  "Click-and-collect et commande sur place."
- No visible controls for category edit / delete / reorder.
- No visible controls for product reorder within a category.
- Product options / variants: the "Options & Variantes" section and
  `+ Ajouter une option` button exist on the product form, but no live
  option fields or persistence flow were observed (no evidence of variants
  being stored or offered on the storefront).
- No Stripe Connect section in `/settings` for free-tier accounts — no
  `charges_enabled` / `payouts_enabled` indicators, no connect CTA.
- No billing / entitlements page, no current-plan summary, no module
  activation UI beyond `mailto:hello@quickarte.fr` on the upsell cards.
- No reservation request UI observed on storefront or dashboard, even
  though the product positioning includes "brunch spots".
- No dedicated analytics / insights route. `/home` shows today's Commandes
  and Revenu; "top items", "peak hours", and the promised "chiffre / produits
  qui marchent / heures fortes" view is not reachable on the tested
  accounts. `home/page.tsx` does have analytics code paths behind
  entitlements — these are just never active for a default account.
- Contact-form submission on the landing page was not exercised; success /
  failure behaviour is unverified.

## Could not test
- **Interactive browser testing mid-session.** The chrome-devtools MCP
  browser would not launch in this environment (Chrome launches fine
  directly from bash, so the MCP sandbox configuration is the blocker).
  I relied on the morning session's complete `.playwright-mcp/` snapshot
  set plus HTTP probing plus source inspection. I could not, for example,
  click a brand-new interaction path or change viewport size in the current
  session.
- Merchant order lifecycle: untestable because `/orders` was gated on the
  only accounts available.
- Stripe Connect onboarding, charges, and payouts: no active Stripe
  section in the tested accounts and no test-mode payout cycle to observe.
- Loyalty program configuration and member management: `/loyalty` is
  `Module non activé` for the tested accounts.
- Restaurant and Autre onboarding seed behaviour: only Boulangerie and
  Café were exercised. Based on the code, non-Boulangerie types should
  produce an empty catalog (confirmed for Café only).
- Product deletion to completion: the edit form exposes "Supprimer cet
  article" but the destructive action wasn't executed.
- Analytics 7-day / 30-day, top items, peak hours, order mix: entitlement
  isn't active on the tested UI, and the code path requires it.
- PWA install prompt / push notifications: not testable without browser
  permission grant and an active entitlement.

## UX / design observations
- Brutalist direction is consistent: sharp borders, `font-mono uppercase
  tracking-widest` labels, monochrome with a single accent, phone-framed
  app shell. The aesthetic reads intentional.
- French is consistent in UI copy except for one concrete leak: the Next
  default 404 at `/fr/catalog/nonexistent-id` ("This page could not be
  found.").
- Onboarding's live slug preview reads `quickarte.app/<slug>` while every
  other URL in the product (dashboard, QR, copy-to-clipboard, storefront)
  uses `quickarte.fr/<slug>`. This creates avoidable doubt at the moment
  the merchant is choosing their slug.
- Mobile-first works for every surface except the product form, where the
  fixed bottom nav intercepts the primary CTAs. This is the single biggest
  "works visually, fails in practice" issue.
- Hiding `Commandes` and `Fidélité` tabs entirely on gated accounts shrinks
  the perceived product and eliminates the upgrade cue. Better to keep the
  tabs visible and route to the upsell card on tap — same conversion
  surface, stronger feature discovery.
- The Boulangerie "Démarrage" prompt showing for a merchant whose catalog
  was just seeded undermines the "instant first value" story that the
  seed itself is trying to deliver.
- Empty category headings (e.g. `Audit Catégorie` with no items) are
  visible on the public storefront. Admin-acceptable; customer-facing, it
  looks like something is missing.

## Unexpected findings
- The storefront checkout works without any Stripe setup: orders default
  to `À régler sur place`, so a merchant without a payments entitlement
  can still receive on-site orders — they just can't see them in
  `/orders` (see blocker above).
- The `/home` KPI grid counts Commandes and computes Revenu even though
  Orders are gated in nav — the dashboard partially reflects data the
  merchant can't drill into.
- Settings has Stripe / notification section code, but it is
  conditionally hidden unless `online_ordering` entitlement is active.
- The public storefront target is the root slug path (`/<slug>`), not
  `/s/<slug>`. QR generation already matches this; the `/s/<slug>`
  convention in the benchmark is not implemented.
- Sentry client instrumentation is active on the production pages
  (traces visible in HTML meta); production error logging exists.

## Recommended next actions
1. Fix the customer/merchant asymmetry: either gate customer checkout on
   the same entitlement as `/orders`, or make `/orders` accessible to any
   merchant whose storefront accepts orders.
2. Fix the mobile product-form layering so `Enregistrer`, `+ Ajouter une
   option`, and delete controls are clickable without keyboard workarounds.
3. Fix the `Démarrage` gate on `/home` so a seeded catalog counts as "ready"
   — branch on catalog non-emptiness, not just order activity.
4. Persist and surface the Google Places address in `/settings`
   (`Emplacement`) and on the storefront header.
5. Fix the Revenu KPI: use `formatAmount` (2 decimals) instead of
   `formatAmountCompact` on `/home`, and put a space between label and
   the `€` unit so it doesn't read "Revenu€".
6. Keep `Commandes` and `Fidélité` visible in bottom nav for all accounts;
   route to the upsell card on tap. Same conversion point, stronger
   discovery.
7. Localise `app/not-found.tsx` (or the merchant-side equivalent) so
   `/fr/catalog/<bad-id>` no longer shows "This page could not be found."
8. Ship the full order manager UI (pending → confirmed → complete, cancel,
   type filter) to whichever surface stays available to merchants once
   checkout is unblocked.
9. Add category edit/delete/reorder and product reorder, or remove those
   capabilities from the landing-page product promise.
10. Decide whether options/variants and click-and-collect are in-scope
    for pilot, and either finish them or hide the stubs / copy.
11. Add a visible billing / entitlements surface and a Stripe Connect
    status section so a pilot merchant can see what's active.
12. Align slug-preview copy in onboarding to `quickarte.fr/<slug>` to
    match the rest of the product.
13. Serve `/favicon.ico` (redirect or file) to silence the console 404.
